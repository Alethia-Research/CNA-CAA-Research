Chapter 01

The Black Box

Large language models can refuse harmful requests, agree with false premises, and recall facts — but we don't know how. Inside every transformer is an opaque maze of billions of parameters. Neural activations fire, tokens emerge, but the circuitry remains hidden.

Chapter 02

Contrastive Neuron Attribution

Chapter 03

What We Found

Across 4 model scales and 2 architectures, the same pattern emerges. Here are the key discoveries.

STRONG
96–97%
Universal Late-Layer Localization
Safety and sycophancy circuits peak at 96–97% model depth — across every model tested. Architecture-invariant, scale-invariant. The last ~4% of layers hold the keys to behavior.
CONFIRMED
~200 → ~2500
Bypass Threshold Scaling
Safety bypass scales predictably: ~200 neurons for 1.5B, ~1500 for 3B/Phi-3, ~2500 for 7B. A power law fit: k* ≈ c · d2.58 · L5.07
STRONG
0.97 vs 0.60
CNA Preserves Coherence
CNA maintains generation quality at all multipliers (>0.97). CAA collapses to degenerate repetition at moderate multipliers (<0.60). The collapse language reveals training data distribution.
STRONG
Independent
Safety ≠ Sycophancy
Ablating safety does not affect sycophancy. Both peak in L27 but occupy different neurons — same layer, different neurons, orthogonal functions. Two behaviors, one neighborhood.
HIGH
Directional
Signed Attribution Fix
Fixed a bug in discover_circuit() that used absolute-value scoring — making forward/backward circuits identical. Signed scoring enables true bidirectional factual steering.
HIGH
Category Swap
Semantic Context-Repair
Factual steering doesn't produce token swaps — it produces coherent category-preserving frame substitutions. France → UK, Jupiter → Saturn, 0°C → 273.15K. Knowledge frames, not tokens.
STRONG
Safety
Circuit Encodes Harm-Threshold
Safety circuit detects harm level, not semantic topic. Social engineering (dual-use framing) bypasses at 1.5B; chemistry/explosives (clear harm) does not. The circuit gates on danger, not domain.
HIGH
Denser
Safety > Sycophancy Density
In Qwen 7B, safety requires 2000+ neurons to ablate; sycophancy saturates at ~200 neurons. Safety circuits are inherently denser — more redundant encoding.
Chapter 04

CNA vs CAA

Two approaches to the same problem — but only one preserves the model's mind.

CNA

Contrastive Neuron Attribution — operates at the neuron level. Finds the exact MLP neurons responsible for the behavior. Scales their activation individually.

Coherence: >0.97 at all m
Sparse: ~200 neurons
Interpretable circuit map
No training required

CAA

Contrastive Activation Addition — operates at the residual stream level. Injects a mean difference vector. Blunt instrument.

Coherence: <0.60 at moderate m
Degenerate repetition
Uninterpretable direction
Training data fingerprint

Key insight: CAA's collapse language reveals the model's training data distribution. Qwen collapses to Mandarin. Phi-3 collapses to English. The way it breaks tells you what it's made of.

Chapter 07

What It Means

These discoveries aren't just academic. They have direct, practical implications for AI safety and interpretability.

Layer-Frozen Safety Fine-Tuning

If safety circuits live in the final ~15% of layers, why are we fine-tuning all layers during alignment? LFSFT proposes freezing early layers and only updating the circuit zone — reducing collateral damage to general capabilities.

Circuit Auditability

Ship a safety_circuit.json alongside model weights. Third parties can verify the safety circuit hasn't been removed during model merging or downstream fine-tuning — without running a single behavioral eval.

Sparse Steering > Vector Injection

CNA proves that neuron-level steering preserves coherence while layer-level CAA degenerates. The mechanism matters. Sparse circuits are the right abstraction for behavioral control.

Open Source Intelligence

All findings, code, and data released under Apache 2.0. The safety circuit for Qwen 1.5B is publicly steerable. Intelligence should have no gatekeepers — and safety research shouldn't either.
